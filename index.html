THE RIVER KELVIN

MOVING TOWARDS A BETTER ENVIRONMENT

Introduction

River Kelvin is a 22-mile river that rises near Banton and flows west through Strathkelvin into the heart of Glasgow, where it joins the River Clyde. In the past, the river was used to power mills and was a source of water for various industries

Several initiatives have aimed to clean up the River Kelvin, including a £15 million sewer upgrade by Scottish Water and ongoing litter picks by groups like Friends of the River Kelvin. Schools and local campaigns such as Upstream Battle® have also raised awareness about river pollution.

Despite these efforts, litter such as plastic waste and flushed items remains a persistent problem, showing that cleanup work alone isn’t enough without wider cultural and systemic change.
